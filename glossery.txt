    <dl>
      <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Heran_Adan" title="Heran Adan">Adan, Heran</a> (ay-DAN,
        HEH-ran)
      </dt>
      <dd>Governor of <a href="/wiki/Baerlon" title="Baerlon">Baerlon</a>.</dd>
      <dt>
        <cite id="Aes_Sedai" style="font-style: normal"
          ><a href="/wiki/Aes_Sedai" title="Aes Sedai">Aes Sedai</a> (EYEZ
          seh-DEYE)</cite
        >
      </dt>
      <dd>
        Wielders of the
        <a href="/wiki/One_Power" title="One Power">One Power</a>. Since the
        <a
          href="/wiki/Time_of_Madness"
          class="mw-redirect"
          title="Time of Madness"
          >Time of Madness</a
        >, all surviving Aes Sedai are women. Widely distrusted and feared, even
        hated, they are blamed by many for the
        <a href="/wiki/Breaking_of_the_World" title="Breaking of the World"
          >Breaking of the World</a
        >, and are generally thought to meddle in the affairs of nations. At the
        same time, few rulers will be without an Aes Sedai advisor, even in
        lands where the existence of such a connection must be kept secret. Used
        as an honorific, so: Sheriam Sedai; and as a high honorific, so: Sheriam
        Aes Sedai. <i>See also</i>
        <a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#Ajah"
          title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
          >Ajah</a
        >;
        <a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#Amyrlin_Seat"
          title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
          >Amyrlin Seat</a
        >
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Age_Lace" class="mw-redirect" title="Age Lace"
          >Age Lace</a
        >
      </dt>
      <dd>
        <i>See</i>
        <a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#Pattern_of_an_Age"
          title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
          >Pattern of an Age</a
        >
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Legends" title="Age of Legends">Age of Legends</a>
      </dt>
      <dd>
        The Age ended by the
        <a
          href="/wiki/War_of_the_Shadow"
          class="mw-redirect"
          title="War of the Shadow"
          >War of the Shadow</a
        >
        and the
        <a href="/wiki/Breaking_of_the_World" title="Breaking of the World"
          >Breaking of the World</a
        >. A time when
        <a href="/wiki/Aes_Sedai" title="Aes Sedai">Aes Sedai</a> performed
        wonders now only dreamed of. <i>See also:</i
        ><a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#Wheel_of_Time"
          title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
          >Wheel of Time</a
        >
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Agelmar_Jagad" title="Agelmar Jagad"
          >Agelmar; Lord Agelmar of House of Jagad</a
        >
        (AGH-el-mar; JAH-gad)
      </dt>
      <dd>
        Lord of <a href="/wiki/Fal_Dara" title="Fal Dara">Fal Dara</a>. His sign
        is three running red foxes.
      </dd>
      <dt><a href="/wiki/Aiel" title="Aiel">Aiel</a> (eye-EEL)</dt>
      <dd>
        The people <a href="/wiki/Aiel_Waste" title="Aiel Waste">Aiel Waste</a>.
        Fierce and hardy. Also called Aielmen. They veil their faces before they
        kill, giving rise to the saying "acting like a black-veiled Aiel" to
        decribe someone who is being violent. Deadly warriors with weapons or
        with nothing but their bare hands, they will not touch a sword. Their
        pipers play them into battle with the music of dances, and Aielmen call
        battle "the Dance".
      </dd>
      <dt><a href="/wiki/Aiel_Waste" title="Aiel Waste">Aiel Waste</a></dt>
      <dd>
        The harsh, rugged and all-but-waterless land east of the
        <a href="/wiki/Spine_of_the_World" title="Spine of the World"
          >Spine of the World</a
        >. Few outsiders venture there, not only because water is almost
        impossible to find for one not born there, but because the Aiel consider
        themselves at war with all other peoples and do not welcome strangers.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <cite id="Ajah" style="font-style: normal"
          ><a href="/wiki/Ajah" title="Ajah">Ajah</a> (AH-jah)</cite
        >
      </dt>
      <dd>
        Societies among the Aes Sedai, to which all Aes Sedai belong. They are
        designated by colors: Blue Ajah, Red Ajah, White Ajah, Green Ajah, Brown
        Ajah, Yellow Ajah, and Gray Ajah. Each follows a specific pjilosophy of
        the use of the
        <a href="/wiki/One_Power" title="One Power">One Power</a> and purposes
        of the Aes Sedai. For example, the Red Ajah bends all its energies to
        finding and gentling men who are attempting to wield the Power. The
        Brown Ajah, on the other hand, forsakes involvment with the world and
        dedicates itself to seeking knowledge. There are rumors (hotly denied,
        and never safely mentioned in front of any Aes Sedai) of a Black Ajah,
        dedicated to serving the
        <a href="/wiki/Dark_One" title="Dark One">Dark One</a>.
      </dd>
      <dt><i>Al Ellisande!</i> (ahlehl-lih-SAHN-dah)</dt>
      <dd>
        In the <a href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a>,
        "For the
        <a
          href="/wiki/Rose_of_the_Sun"
          class="mw-redirect"
          title="Rose of the Sun"
          >Rose of the Sun</a
        >!"
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <i><a href="/wiki/Aldieb" title="Aldieb">Aldieb</a></i> (ahl-DEEB)
      </dt>
      <dd>
        In the <a href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a>,
        "West Wind," the wind that brings the spring rains.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Nynaeve_al%27Meara" title="Nynaeve al'Meara"
          >al'Meara, Nynaeve</a
        >
        (ahl-MEER-ah, NIGH-neev)
      </dt>
      <dd>
        The <a href="/wiki/Wisdom" title="Wisdom">Wisdom</a> of
        <a href="/wiki/Emond%27s_Field" title="Emond's Field">Emond's Field</a>.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Rand_al%27Thor" title="Rand al'Thor">al'Thor, Rand</a>
        (ahl-THOR, RAND)
      </dt>
      <dd>
        A young farmer and sheepherder from the
        <a href="/wiki/Two_Rivers" title="Two Rivers">Two Rivers</a>.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Egwene_al%27Vere" title="Egwene al'Vere"
          >al'Vere, Egwene</a
        >
        (ahl-VEER, eh-GWAIN)
      </dt>
      <dd>
        Youngest daughter of the innkeeper in
        <a href="/wiki/Emond%27s_Field" title="Emond's Field">Emond's Field</a>.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <cite id="Amyrlin_Seat" style="font-style: normal"
          ><a href="/wiki/Amyrlin_Seat" title="Amyrlin Seat">Amyrlin Seat</a>
          (AHM-ehr-lin)</cite
        >
      </dt>
      <dd>
        (1) The title of the leader of the
        <a href="/wiki/Aes_Sedai" title="Aes Sedai">Aes Sedai</a>. Elected for
        life by the
        <a href="/wiki/Hall_of_the_Tower" title="Hall of the Tower"
          >Hall of the Tower</a
        >, the highest council of the Aes Sedai, which consists of three
        representatives from each of the seven
        <a href="/wiki/Ajah" title="Ajah">Ajahs</a>. The Amyrlin Seat has,
        throtetically at least, almost supreme authority among the Aes Sedai.
        She ranks as the equal of a king or queen.
      </dd>
      <dd>(2) The throne upon which the leader of the Aes Sedai sits.</dd>
      <dt><a href="/wiki/Andor" title="Andor">Andor</a> (AN-door)</dt>
      <dd>
        The realm within which the
        <a href="/wiki/Two_Rivers" title="Two Rivers">Two Rivers</a> lies. The
        sign of Andor is a rampant white lion on a field of red.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <i><a href="/wiki/Angreal" title="Angreal">angreal</a></i>
        (ahn-gree-AHL)
      </dt>
      <dd>
        A very rare object which allows anyone capable of channeling the
        <a href="/wiki/One_Power" title="One Power">One Power</a> to handle a
        greater amount of the Power than would be safely possible unaided.
        Remnants of the
        <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Legends" title="Age of Legends">Age of Legends</a
        >, the means of their making is no longer known.
        <i
          >See also
          <a
            href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#sa'angreal"
            title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
            >sa'angreal</a
          ></i
        >
      </dd>
      <dt><a href="/wiki/Arafel" title="Arafel">Arafel</a> (AH-rah-fehl)</dt>
      <dd>
        One of the
        <a href="/wiki/Borderlands" title="Borderlands">Borderlands</a>. The
        sign of Arafel is three white roses on a field of red, quartered with
        three red roses on a field of white.
      </dd>
      <dt><a href="/wiki/Aram" title="Aram">Aram</a> (AY-ram)</dt>
      <dd>
        A young man of the
        <i><a href="/wiki/Tuatha%27an" title="Tuatha'an">Tuatha'an</a></i
        >.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <i
          ><a href="/wiki/Avendesora" class="mw-redirect" title="Avendesora"
            >Avendesora</a
          ></i
        >
        (Ah-vehn-deh-SO-rah)
      </dt>
      <dd>
        In the <a href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a>,
        "the Tree of Life." Mentioned in many stories and legends.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Perrin_Aybara" title="Perrin Aybara">Aybara, Perrin</a>
        (ay-BAHR-ah, PEHR-rihn)
      </dt>
      <dd>
        A young blacksmith's apprentice from
        <a href="/wiki/Emond%27s_Field" title="Emond's Field">Emond's Field</a>.
      </dd>

        inevitably bringing ill-fortune at best, disaster at worst. For that
        reason many euphemisms are used, among them the Dark One, Father of
        Lies, Sightblinder, Lord of the Grave, Shepherd of the Night,
        Heartsbane, Heartfang, Grassburner, and Leafblighter. Someone who seems
        to be inviting ill fortune is often said to be "naming the Dark One."

      <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Five_Powers" title="Five Powers">Five Powers, the</a>
      </dt>
      <dd>
        There are threads to the
        <a href="/wiki/One_Power" title="One Power">One Power</a>, and each
        person who can channel the One Power can usually grasp some threads
        better than others. These threads are named according to the sorts of
        things that can be done using them — Earth, Air, Fire, Water, and Spirit
        — and are called the Five Powers. Any wielder of the One Power will have
        a greater degree of strength with one, or possibly two, of these, and
        lesser strength in the others. Some few may have great strength with
        three, but since the Age of Legends no one has had great strength with
        all five. Even then this was extremely rare. The degree of strength can
        vary greatly between individuals, so that some who can channel are much
        stronger than others. Performing certain acts with the One Power
        requires ability in one or more of the Five Powers. For example,
        starting or controlling a fire requires Fire, and affecting the weather
        requires Air and Water, while Healing requires Water and Spirit. While
        Spirit was found equally in men and women, great ability with Earth
        and/or Fire was found much more often among men, with Water and/or Air
        among women. There were exceptions, but it was so often so that Earth
        and Fire came to be regarded as male Powers, Air and Water as female.
        Generally, no ability is considered stronger than any other, though
        there is a saying among Aes Sedai: “There is no rock so strong that
        water and wind cannot wear it away, no fire so fierce that water cannot
        quench it or wind snuff it out.” It should be noted this saying came
        into use long after the last male Aes Sedai was dead. Any equivalent
        saying among male
        <a href="/wiki/Aes_Sedai" title="Aes Sedai">Aes Sedai</a> is long lost.
      </dd>

      </dd>

      <dd>
        Name given to thirteen of the most powerful

      </dt>
      <dd>

        <a href="/wiki/Aes_Sedai" title="Aes Sedai">Aes Sedai</a>, of shutting
        off a male who can channel from the
        <a href="/wiki/One_Power" title="One Power">One Power</a>. This is
        necessary because any man who learns to channel will go insane from the
        taint upon saidin and will almost certainly do horrible things with the
        Power in his madness. A man who has been gentled can still sense the
        True Source, but he cannot touch it. Whatever madness has come before
        gentling is arrested by the act of gentling, but not cured by it, and if
        it is done soon enough death can be averted.

      <dd>
        The power drawn from the
        <a href="/wiki/True_Source" class="mw-redirect" title="True Source"
          >True Source</a
        >. The vast majority of people are completely unable to learn to channel
        the One Power. A very small number can be taught to channel, and an even
        tinier number have the ability inborn. For these few there is no need to
        be taught; they will touch the True Source and channel the Power whether
        they want to or not, perhaps without even realizing what they are doing.
        This inborn ability usually manifests itself in late adolescence or
        early adulthood. If control is not taught, or self-learned (extremely
        difficult, with a success rate of only one in four), death is certain.
        Since the
        <a
          href="/wiki/Time_of_Madness"
          class="mw-redirect"
          title="Time of Madness"
          >time of Madness</a
        >, no man has been able to channel the Power without eventually going
        completely, horribly mad; and then, even if he has learned some control,
        dying from a wasting sickness which causes the sufferer to rot alive—a
        sickness caused, as is the madness, by the Dark One's
        <a href="/wiki/Taint" title="Taint">taint on saidin</a>. For a woman the
        death that comes without control of the Power is less horrible, but it
        is death just the same.
        <a href="/wiki/Aes_Sedai" title="Aes Sedai">Aes Sedai</a> search for
        girls with the inborn ability as much to save their lives as to increase
        Aes Sedai numbers, and for men with it in order to stop the terrible
        things they inevitably do with the Power in their madness.
        <i>See also</i>
        <a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#channel"
          title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
          >channel</a
        >;
        <a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#Time_of_Madness"
          title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
          >Time of Madness</a
        >;
        <a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#True_Source"
          title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
          >True Source</a
        >.

        deceitful and treacherous, they can be trusted only by those they fear.
        They are omnivorous and will eat any kind of meat, including human flesh
        and the flesh of other Trollocs. Largely of human origin, they are able
        to interbreed with humankind, but the offspring are usually stillborn,
        and those which are not often fail to survive. They are divided into
        tribe-like bands, chief among them being the Ahf'frait, Al'ghol,
        Bhan'sheen, Dha'vol, Dhai'mon, Dhjin'nen, Ghar'ghael, Ghob'hlin,
        Gho'hlem, Ghraem'lan, Ko'bal, and the Kno'mon.
      </dd>
      <dt>

            >True Source</a
          ></cite
        >
      </dt>
      <dd>

        <a href="/wiki/Wheel_of_Time" title="Wheel of Time">Wheel of Time</a>.

          ><a href="/wiki/Saidin" title="Saidin">saidin</a></i
        >) and a female half (<i
          ><a href="/wiki/Saidar" title="Saidar">saidar</a></i
        >), which work at the same time with and against each other. Only a man
        can draw on <i>saidin</i>, only a woman on <i>saidar</i>. Since the
        beginning of the
        <a
          href="/wiki/Time_of_Madness"
          class="mw-redirect"
          title="Time of Madness"
          >Time of Madness</a
        >, <i>saidin</i> has been
        <a href="/wiki/Taint" title="Taint">tainted</a> by the Dark One's touch.
        <i>See also</i>
        <a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#One_Power"
          title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
          >One Power</a
        >.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <cite style="font-style: normal" id="Tuatha'an"

          (too-AH-thah-AHN)</cite
        >
      </dt>
      <dd>

        who live in brightly painted wagons and follow a totally pacifist
        philosophy called the
        <a href="/wiki/Way_of_the_Leaf" title="Way of the Leaf"
          >Way of the Leaf</a
        >. Things mended by Tinkers are often better than new, but the
        <i>Tuatha'an</i> are shunned by many villages because of stories that
        they steal children and try to convert young people to their beliefs.
      </dd>

      <dt>
        <a

          title="War of the Hundred Years"
          >War of the Hundred Years</a
        >
      </dt>
      <dd>
        A series of overlapping wars among constantly shifting alliances,
        precipitated by the death of
        <a href="/wiki/Artur_Hawkwing" title="Artur Hawkwing">Artur Hawkwing</a>
        and the resulting struggle for his empire. It lasted from
        <a href="/wiki/FY_994" class="mw-redirect" title="FY 994">FY 994</a> to
        <a href="/wiki/FY_1117" class="mw-redirect" title="FY 1117">FY 1117</a>.
        The war depopulated large parts of the lands between the
        <a href="/wiki/Aryth_Ocean" title="Aryth Ocean">Aryth Ocean</a> and the
        <a href="/wiki/Aiel_Waste" title="Aiel Waste">Aiel Waste</a>, from the
        <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Storms" title="Sea of Storms">Sea of Storms</a> to
        the <a href="/wiki/Great_Blight" title="Great Blight">Great Blight</a>.
        So great was the destruction that only fragmentary records of the time
        remain. The empire of Artur Hawkwing was pulled apart, and the nations
        of the present day were formed.
      </dd>
      <dt>
        <a
          href="/wiki/War_of_the_Shadow"
          class="mw-redirect"
          title="War of the Shadow"
          >War of the Shadow</a
        >
      </dt>
      <dd>
        Also known as the War of Power, it ended the
        <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Legends" title="Age of Legends">Age of Legends</a
        >. It began shortly after the attempt to free the
        <a href="/wiki/Dark_One" title="Dark One">Dark One</a>, and soon
        involved the whole world. In a world where even the memory of war had
        been forgotten, every facet of war was rediscovered, often twisted by
        the Dark One's touch on the World, and the
        <a href="/wiki/One_Power" title="One Power">One Power</a> was used as a
        weapon. The war was ended by the resealing of the Dark One into his
        prison.
      </dd>
      <dt><a href="/wiki/Warder" title="Warder">Warder</a></dt>
      <dd>
        A warrior bonded to an
        <a href="/wiki/Aes_Sedai" title="Aes Sedai">Aes Sedai</a>. The bonding
        is a thing of the
        <a href="/wiki/One_Power" title="One Power">One Power</a>, and by it he
        gains such gifts as quick healing, the ability to go long periods
        without food, water or rest, and the ability to sense the taint of the
        <a href="/wiki/Dark_One" title="Dark One">Dark One</a> at a distance. So
        long as a Warder lives, the Aes Sedai to whom he is bonded knows he is
        alive however far away he is, and when he dies she will know the moment
        and manner of his death. While most
        <a href="/wiki/Ajah" title="Ajah">Ajahs</a> believe an Aes Sedai may
        have one Warder bonded to her at a time, the
        <a href="/wiki/Red_Ajah" title="Red Ajah">Red Ajah</a> refuses to bond
        any Warders at all, while the
        <a href="/wiki/Green_Ajah" title="Green Ajah">Green Ajah</a> believes an
        Aes Sedai may bond as many Warders as she wishes. Ethically the Warder
        must accede to the bonding, but it has been known to be done
        involuntarily. What the Aes Sedai gain from the bonding is a
        closely-held secret. <i>See also</i>
        <a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#Aes_Sedai"
          title="The Eye of the World/Glossary"
          >Aes Sedai</a
        >.

            >Wheel of time, the</a

        >
      </dt>
      <dd>
        Time is a wheel with seven spokes, each spoke an Age. As the Wheel
        turns, the Ages come and go, each leaving memories that fade to legend,
        then to myth, and are forgotten by the time that Age comes again. The
        Pattern of an Age is slightly different each time an Age comes, and each
        time it is subject to greater change, but each time it is the same Age.

      <dt><a href="/wiki/Wisdom" title="Wisdom">Wisdom</a></dt>
      <dd>
        In villages, a woman chosen by the Women's Circle to sit in the Circle
        for her knowledge of such things as healing and foretelling the weather,
        as well as for common, good sense. A position of great responsibility
        and authority, both actual and implied. She is generally considered the
        equal of the Mayor, and in some villages his superior. Unlike the Mayor,
        she is chosen for life, and it is very rare for a Wisdom to be removed
        from office before her death. Almost traditionally in conflict with the
        Mayor. <i>See also</i>

          ></cite
        >
      </dt>
      <dd>
        A group of women elected by the women of a village, responsible for
        deciding such matters as are considered solely women's responsibility
        (for example, when to plant the crops and when to harvest). Equal in
        authority to the Village Council, with clearly-delineated lines and
        areas of responsibility. Often at odds with the Village Council.
        <i>See also</i>
        <a
          href="/wiki/The_Eye_of_the_World/Glossary#Village_Council"
